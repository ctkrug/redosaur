What ReDoSaur does

ReDoSaur is a browser tool that checks whether a regular expression is vulnerable to catastrophic backtracking, the bug behind regular-expression denial of service (ReDoS). Instead of matching your pattern against a list of known-bad shapes the way most linters do, it parses the pattern into a syntax tree and runs it through an instrumented backtracking engine, the same evaluation strategy used by PCRE, Python's re module, JavaScript's RegExp, and Java's Pattern. It counts every backtracking step, builds a worst-case input designed to make the pattern fail as slowly as possible, and shows the step count climbing in real time.

Because the verdict comes from measured step growth rather than the shape of the source text, it catches dangerous patterns that shape-based tools miss and clears safe patterns that only look risky. When a pattern is flagged, ReDoSaur proposes an equivalent rewrite that removes the ambiguity and re-runs it against the same input, so you watch the step count drop from millions to a handful.

How to use it

Type or paste a pattern into the field at the top, or pick one of the example chips, and press Run. ReDoSaur shows the worst-case input it generated, the backtracking step count, and a risk verdict of Safe, Suspicious, or Catastrophic. If the pattern is risky, press Suggest fix to see a safer version measured side by side. Nothing is uploaded: parsing and simulation run entirely in WebAssembly in your browser, so patterns from private code never leave your machine.

FAQ

What is ReDoS?
Regular-expression denial of service. A pattern with nested or ambiguous quantifiers can take exponential time to fail on a crafted input, letting a single request pin a CPU core and stall a server.
Which regex features does ReDoSaur support?
Literals, character classes and shorthands (\d, \w, \s and negations), concatenation, alternation, the *, +, ?, and {m,n} quantifiers, capturing and non-capturing groups, and the ^ and $ anchors.
Does my regex get sent to a server?
No. The engine is compiled to WebAssembly and runs locally in your browser. No pattern you enter is transmitted or stored.
Is a "Catastrophic" verdict definitely exploitable?
It means the step count grows explosively on generated worst-case input, the signature of catastrophic backtracking. Whether it is reachable in your app depends on whether an attacker controls the matched string, so treat it as a strong signal to review, not a proof of a live exploit.
How is this different from a regex linter?
A linter reads your pattern and guesses from its shape. ReDoSaur runs the pattern and measures the actual blowup, which avoids both the false alarms and the blind spots of shape matching.

No regex ever leaves your browser. Parsing and simulation run entirely in WASM. See docs/VISION.md and docs/BACKLOG.md.
